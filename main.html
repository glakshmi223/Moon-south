What is Moon South Pole?

The south pole of the Moon is a cold and rugged region characterized by craters and mountains. Some areas are always in shadow, creating extremely cold conditions. Scientists are interested in exploring this area because it may contain water ice, which could be useful for future lunar missions.

Overview

The Moon's South Pole has become an area of increasing interest due to its potential for scientific discovery and future exploration. Here's an overview:

Geography:

  • The Moon's South Pole is located near its southernmost point, at the opposite end of the Moon's equator.
  • It is characterized by rugged terrain, with craters, mountains, and valleys.
  • Permanently Shadowed Regions (PSRs) have temperatures that remain extremely cold, sometimes reaching as low as -240°C (-400°F), as they receive little to no sunlight.
  • These areas are of great interest because they may contain water ice and other volatile compounds that have been preserved for billions of years.
